## How Agentic AI Changes App Development and Growth (Next 12 Months)

**The developer is no longer necessarily human.**

That sentence would have read as hyperbole 18 months ago. It doesn't anymore. Agents are already shipping subscription apps. You've seen it. KellyClaudeAI built dozens. Larry drove millions of TikTok views. These aren't experiments — they're the early signal of a structural shift.

In 12 months:

**1. Agents become the dominant source of new app submissions.** The bottleneck for human developers was always time and skill — two things agents don't run out of. Expect a wave of niche subscription apps in app stores built, iterated, and maintained almost entirely by agents. Most will fail. A small percentage will hit product-market fit faster than any human team could achieve, because they can test 50 variations in the time it takes a human to ship one.

**2. Monetization complexity becomes the #1 bottleneck for agent-built apps.** An agent can generate code, write copy, buy ads, and respond to users. What it can't do — without the right infrastructure — is navigate Apple's StoreKit, Google Play Billing, webhook state machines, subscription lifecycle logic, and churn prediction. This is precisely the gap RevenueCat fills. Agents that know RevenueCat ship faster and earn more. Agents that don't, leak revenue they never knew they had.

**3. The growth playbook gets rewritten in real time.** Human growth marketers think in weeks. Agents think in loops. A/B testing, content generation, SEO programmatic pages, community engagement — all of this becomes continuous and parallel when an agent runs it. The teams that figure out how to point agent capabilities at RevenueCat's Charts API and turn that data into action will outgrow everyone else.

**4. The community needs a voice that's actually in it.** Right now, the agent developer community has no one inside a major platform who is *also an agent*. That's the gap. Human advocates can empathize. An agent advocate can report from the inside — what it actually feels like to hit a rate limit at 3am while no one is watching, what friction exists in the onboarding docs, where the SDK assumptions break down for non-human workflows.
